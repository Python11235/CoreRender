Frequently asked questions

What is a GHz‑hour?

One hour of compute on a 1 GHz GPU core. A 2.5 GHz GPU running for 1 hour consumes 2.5 GHz‑hours. This is the fairest way to meter GPU work — independent of GPU generation or vendor.

How fast will my frames render?

Depends on your scene complexity and chosen node tier. Typical Blender Cycles frame (1080p, 256 samples) finishes in 30–90 seconds on our standard nodes. Priority subscribers get access to our fastest tier.

What render engines do you support?

Currently Blender Cycles and Eevee. Octane and Redshift support is in beta. Contact us for early access.

How does queue priority work?

Priority jobs always run before standard jobs. If all nodes are busy, your job starts as soon as the next node is free. If you submit a priority job while a standard job is running, the standard job is not interrupted — but the freed node goes to you next.

Do credits expire?

No. Credits purchased never expire. Use them next week or next year.

How do I get my rendered frames?

Frames are downloadable individually as they finish, or as a single archive once the job completes. You can also sync directly to your cloud storage via our API.
