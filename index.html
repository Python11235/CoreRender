FAQ

Common questions.

What is a GHz·h and how is it calculated?

One GHz·h is one hour of compute on a 1 GHz GPU core. The Arc B580 runs at around 2.8 GHz boost, so one hour on it costs ~2.8 GHz·h. Billing is per-second — a 40-second frame costs 40 seconds of GPU time, not a full minute.

Do I need to pack my .blend before uploading?

Yes. Go to File → External Data → Pack Resources in Blender first. If your scene references unpacked external textures, the render will come out black or missing materials. The uploader will warn you about missing data blocks.

What happens to my files?

Uploaded scenes and rendered frames are stored locally and deleted automatically after 7 days. I don't share, sell, or look at your files. There's a delete button in the dashboard if you want them gone immediately.

Can I use custom add-ons or Python scripts?

Not currently. The render node runs a clean Blender install. Geometry Nodes, built-in modifiers, and stock shaders all work fine. Third-party add-ons that hook into the render pipeline aren't supported, and arbitrary Python execution is disabled for security.

What Blender version do you run?

Currently Blender 4.2 LTS. I'll update to new LTS releases as they stabilise. If you're on a different version and getting weird results, let me know — I keep one older build available for backwards compatibility.

What if the render crashes or looks wrong?

Credits for failed frames are refunded automatically within a few minutes. If output looks wrong and the cause was on my end, open a support ticket and I'll refund manually. If the issue is in your scene file, I can't refund — but I'll tell you what went wrong.

Why is there a weekly refill cap?

Free credits accrue up to 50 GHz·h maximum. This keeps the free tier sustainable on my hardware. If you haven't used the service in weeks you won't suddenly have 100+ hours queued. Paid credits are separate and have no cap.

Is there a job size limit?

Upload limit is 2 GB per scene file. No hard limit on frame count, but very long jobs may be split into batches automatically. VRAM-heavy scenes approaching 10–11 GB may fail on the B580 — the P4 is capped at 8 GB and won't handle them.
