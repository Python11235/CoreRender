FAQ

Common questions.

What is a GHz·h and how is it calculated?

One GHz·h is one hour of compute on a 1 GHz GPU core. The Arc B580 runs at around 2.8 GHz boost, so one hour on it costs approximately 2.8 GHz·h. Billing is per-second — if your frame takes 40 seconds, you pay for 40 seconds of GPU time, not a full minute or hour.

Do I have to pack my .blend file before uploading?

Yes. Go to File → External Data → Pack Resources in Blender before exporting. If your scene references external textures or assets that aren't packed, the render will come out black or missing materials. The uploader will warn you if it detects missing data blocks but can't fix them automatically.

What happens to my files?

Uploaded scenes and rendered frames are stored locally on my server and deleted automatically after 7 days. I don't share, sell, or look at your files. If you want something removed immediately, there's a delete button in the dashboard.

Can I render with custom add-ons or Python scripts?

Not currently. The render node runs a clean Blender install. Geometry Nodes, built-in modifiers, and stock shaders all work fine. Third-party add-ons that hook into the render pipeline aren't supported — and for security reasons, arbitrary Python execution is disabled.

What Blender version do you run?

Currently Blender 4.2 LTS. I'll bump to new LTS releases as they stabilise. If you're on a different version and getting weird results, let me know — I keep one older build available for backwards compatibility.

What if the render crashes or produces wrong output?

Credits for failed frames are refunded automatically within a few minutes. If output looks wrong (wrong colours, missing objects, etc.) and the cause was on my end, open a support ticket and I'll refund manually. If the issue is in your scene file, I can't refund — but I'll try to tell you why it failed.

What's the weekly refill cap and why?

Free credits accrue up to a maximum of 50 GHz·h — so if you haven't used the service for a few weeks, you won't suddenly have 100+ hours queued up. This keeps the free tier sustainable on my hardware. Paid credits are separate and have no cap.

Is there a job size limit?

Upload limit is 2 GB per scene file. There's no hard limit on frame count or render duration, but extremely long jobs (multi-day estimates) may be split into batches automatically. Very VRAM-heavy scenes (approaching 10–11 GB) may fail on the B580 — the P4 is limited to 8 GB and won't handle them at all.
